Pueblo's Dual Housing Era

Older Pueblo Home? Your Pipes Are Telling You Something.

Pueblo's housing stock divides into two distinct eras, and each carries different plumbing risks. Historic properties in Bessemer, Mesa Junction, Downtown, and Highland Park (many dating to the CF&I Steel City era)run galvanized supply lines and cast iron drains that corrode from the inside out. The 1960s–80s tract homes across Belmont, Lakeview, Country Club, and Sunset Park switched to copper supply lines. Those copper systems are now 40 to 60 years old. With Pueblo Water running at roughly 180 mg/L hardness, the corrosion timeline accelerates. Pinhole leaks in this era of Pueblo home are not a question of whether. They are a question of when.

1900s–1950s Historic

Bessemer, Mesa Junction, Downtown, Highland Park, galvanized supply, cast iron drain, basement/crawlspace construction

1960s–1980s Tract

Belmont, Lakeview, Country Club, Sunset Park, copper supply lines now 40–60 years old, slab-on-grade foundations

1990s+ Newer Build

Pueblo West, Aberdeen, newer North Side — PEX or copper, slab-on-grade, expansive clay soil risk remains
